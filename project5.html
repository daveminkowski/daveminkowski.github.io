Safe Internet Browsing Techniques:

Safe browsing is a oft-discussed topic in my household - we have 3 boys aged 11-14, each one an online video game and internet "connoisseur" (junkie). Some of the behavior we've been trying to enforce involves creating secure passwords, browsing only trusted sites and refusing to share/protecting any personal or financial information. That's a small sample of our internet discussion topics at home, but the 3 links below can expand on other safe browsing tips and techniques:

  1. MIT's 'Top Ten Safe Computing Tips'

    2. MIT reminds us to physically secure our computers, our connections AND our data.

  2. Mozilla's 'How to stay safe on the web'

    3. Despite my reluctance to leave my perfect little Google-integrated bubble behind, Mozilla Firefox is, in my estimation, the leader in mainstream browser security through it's library of plugins and extensions.

  3. GetSafeOnline.org

    4. GetSafeOnline is a website dedicated to identifying potential risks and learning how to avoid them. It covers fundamental technical safeguards like privacy and firewalls but expands pretty deeply into topics like cyberbullying and safe disposal of your smartphone.

To further illustrate how your browser might be giving information away without your consent, the table below has been populated with information about your browser/computer. Involuntary surrendering your software version, OS or location presents a potential vulnerability.

This script is pretty simple, it colors alternating rows of the table and populates the value column with your browser data.

Browser & Screen Object Properties:

Object Name: Description: Value From Your Browser:
navigator.appName Browser name
navigator.appVersion Browser version
navigator.onLine Whether the browser has a network connection
navigator.platform OS running the browser
navigator.userAgent Information about browser compatibility
screen.width Height of the display screen
screen.height Width of the display screen
screen.pixelDepth Resolution in bits per pixel
